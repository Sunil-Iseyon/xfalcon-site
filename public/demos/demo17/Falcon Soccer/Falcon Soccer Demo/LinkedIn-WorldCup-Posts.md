Pre-1970: 0% (substitutions weren't even allowed)

## POST 3 — Semi-Finals Outscore Group Stages

"The deeper you go in a tournament, the tighter the games get."

Every football pundit says this.
The data says they're WRONG.

We analyzed goal-scoring across every World Cup stage:

Group Stage: 2.72 goals/match
Round of 16: 2.56 goals/match (OK, fits the theory so far…)
Quarter-Finals: 2.88 goals/match (wait)
Semi-Finals: 3.62 goals/match (WHAT?)
Third-Place Match: 3.94 goals/match (chaos)

Semi-finals produce 33% MORE goals than group stages. Not fewer. MORE.

Why? Our hypothesis: By the semis, two elite attacking teams face each other with nothing to hide. No parking the bus. No playing for a draw. Both teams HAVE to go for it — because there's no second chance.

And third-place matches? Pure entertainment. Zero pressure, maximum attacking freedom. Nearly 4 goals per game.

So when you're planning your World Cup 2026 viewing schedule — don't sleep on the semis and third-place match. That's where the fireworks live.
